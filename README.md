# Vulnerability Management for Policy Design (VMPD)

A diagnostic methodology for identifying structural vulnerabilities in governance regimes.

## What is VMPD?

VMPD is a methodology for analysing policy and governance regimes by examining the conditions of their production and operation, rather than just the policy text itself. It surfaces structural failures that other policy analysis methods miss: conditions the regime depends on but cannot see, actors held accountable for outcomes they have no role in shaping, ambiguity that protects drafters at the cost of the governed, pattern recognition functions assumed without being assigned, and recourse mechanisms that exist on paper but cannot be reached in practice.

The methodology originates in the operational practice of cybersecurity vulnerability management and is translated, through analogical reasoning, into a diagnostic for governance.

## Who is this for?

Policy analysts, governance researchers, regulatory practitioners, and civil society organisations who need to surface structural vulnerabilities in governance regimes — whether to inform drafting, to advocate for affected parties, or to compare regimes across jurisdictions.

You do not need to know how to code to use this repository. The notebooks are designed as structured forms: you fill in fields describing the regime and your analytical judgments, and the notebook produces scored outputs and visualisations.

## How to use this repository

1. Read the methodology document in `methodology/VMPD_methodology.md` to understand what VMPD does and how it works.
2. Open `notebooks/00_start_here.ipynb` for an orientation on how to use the notebooks.
3. Look through `notebooks/02_worked_example_pipeline_attestation.ipynb` to see a completed VMPD analysis.
4. When you're ready to analyse your own regime, copy `notebooks/01_template_blank.ipynb` and fill it in.
